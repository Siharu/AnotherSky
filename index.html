ANOTHER SKY

four years. maybe more.

// WNCORE field log — entry 0 It's been four years since the sky broke open over Kathmandu and the rain came down wrong. Not weather. A wound.

You've been asleep since then — or something like asleep. Nine people you knew are gone from every record that should hold them. You don't remember their names. You should remember their names.

Somewhere out there, a relay tower is still broadcasting. Find the signal. Find the photographs. Find what was taken from you.

Don't look at what's in the rain.
